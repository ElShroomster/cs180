Part 1: Selfie: The Wrong Way vs. The Right Way

For this part, we had to take a selfie of ourselves from different perspectives, namely close up versus far away. In all the pictures, we wanted our face to be the same size despite how close we were to the camera. The pictures should get better and better as we step further away and zoom in.

This was quite a decent result, however I noticed that the more we had to zoom, my iPhone decided to pick a camera with differing levels of saturation, leading to my face looking more and more orange. Also, we can see that the second photo looks quite a bit better than the first.

Thinking about why this is, I learned in cs 184 that if we want to keep our subject the same size while also having to step back, we have to adjust the f-stop of our camera, which directly leads to us changing the focal length. The closer we are to the image, the larger the FOV we need, meaning that our focal length is much less. This causes a distortion commonly seen in fisheye cameras where the perspective gets messed up.
